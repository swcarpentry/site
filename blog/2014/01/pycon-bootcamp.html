We are pleased to announce that registration is now open for our largest event ever: on April 14-15, 2014, we will run three workshops in parallel in association with PyCon 2014 in Montreal, as well as a master class in next-generation sequencing and an introduction to R for Python programmers.

The regular workshop is our largest ever, so we're splitting it into three rooms for learners at different levels. We'll assign you to the most suitable level (based on a survey we'll send out before the bootcamp) so you can be sure the material won't go over your head (or under your feet). It will take place at the spectacular Palais des Congrès in Montreal's International District, close to the Montreal business district, Chinatown, Old Montreal, and is connected to the Montreal Metro's Place-d'Armes station. Please use this link to register.

We're also running two special one-day classes at the same time. Monday's will be taught by Prof. Titus Brown, who will explore next-generation sequencing (NGS) and other topics in bioinformatics using Python. Tuesday's, which will be taught by Prof. Ramnath Vaidyanathan, will be an introduction to R for Python programmers.

In addition to the tools and techniques you'll learn in these workshops, this is an opportunity to meet more than 150 other scientists who are using the same tools and facing the same challenges as you. We encourage you to sign up in pairs or groups so that you have friends to work with at the bootcamp, and can support one another when you're back at your desk applying what you've learned.

For more information, please contact us at {{site.contact}} or check out our web site. Please have a look at the Montreal-Python website. They are organizing regular events like workshops and a Project Night where you'll be able to practice your Python skills; you can find out more on their mailing list.
