One of the first steps in organizing a workshop is often finding someone who will provide the space or sponsor the costs. We have used variations on the short pitch below to introduce Software Carpentry to people and explain why they should back it. Suggestions, improvements, and experience reports are very welcome.

Hello [NAME],

We would like to organize a [Data Carpentry|Software Carpentry] workshop at [INSTITUTION], and were wondering if you would be willing to help. Data Carpentry and Software Carpentry are non-profit organizations whose aim is to teach researchers in a wide variety of fields the data analysis and programmin skills they need to get more done in less time and with less pain.

Workshops typically run for two full days or four half-days, and about 40 learners can take part. The instructors (who are researchers themselves) are all volunteers who have gone through an intensive course on modern teaching methods, so the only cost will be their travel and accommodation, plus a $2500 administration fee to offset instructor training, curriculum development, and so on. The lessons typically cover [DELETE AS APPROPRIATE]:

Data Carpentry

  • Organizing data in spreadsheets and databases
  • Cleaning data with OpenRefine
  • Analyzing and visualizing data with R or Python

Software Carpentry

  • Automating tasks with the Unix shell
  • Version control with Git and GitHub
  • Modular programming with Python, R, or MATLAB

Based on feedback from past participants and some small studies, we believe this training can save participants half a day to a day a week for the rest of their careers. They will be able to do the things they used to do faster and more accurately, but will also be able to tackle new challenges and pursue new research directions. If this sounds interesting, we'd welcome a chance to talk about ways and means.

Thanks,

[NAME]
